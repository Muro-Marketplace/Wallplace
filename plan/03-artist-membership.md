# Section 3: Artist Membership Strategy

## Guiding Principle

Wallspace is a professional commercial channel, not a portfolio site, not a vanity listing, not a social network. Every decision in this section is designed to make artists treat Wallspace the way they would treat gallery representation: as a serious business relationship with real commercial expectations on both sides.

---

## Application and Selection

### Should Artists Apply Before Joining?

Yes. Mandatory application before membership. No self-serve sign-up.

Reasons:
- Quality control from day one. Every artist on the platform has been reviewed.
- Scarcity signal. "Apply to join" positions Wallspace as selective and professional.
- Prevents AI spam and hobbyist clutter before it starts.
- Gives Wallspace data on the applicant pool (styles, mediums, price points) to inform curation.
- Allows the founder to personally vet every artist in v1, building relationships and understanding the supply base.

### How Selective Should Wallspace Be?

Moderately selective. Wallspace is not a blue-chip gallery, but it is not open-access either.

**Target acceptance rate: 40-60% of applicants.**

This is selective enough to maintain quality and create perceived exclusivity, but broad enough to build a viable artist roster. The filter is not "gallery-level prestige" -- it is "commercially viable work that venues and their customers would want on their walls."

### How to Prevent Clutter with Weak Work or AI Spam

Five-layer quality defence:

1. **Paywall.** Monthly membership fee deters casual uploaders and spammers.
2. **Application review.** Every portfolio is manually reviewed before acceptance.
3. **Portfolio minimum.** Artists must submit 10-20 works that demonstrate consistent quality and a coherent style.
4. **Originality requirement.** All work must be original. AI-generated work is not accepted. Artists sign a declaration of originality in the membership agreement.
5. **Ongoing curation.** Wallspace reserves the right to decline specific pieces for venue placement even after an artist is accepted. Membership grants access to the platform, not automatic placement of every submitted work.

### Standards for Accepting Artists

An artist is accepted if they meet ALL of the following:

- **Technical competence.** Work demonstrates professional-level execution (for photographers: composition, exposure, post-processing; for visual artists: technique, finish quality).
- **Coherent body of work.** Portfolio shows a recognisable style or thematic consistency, not a random collection.
- **Commercial viability.** Work is the kind that real people would hang in their homes or that venue owners would want on their walls. This is subjective but critical. Highly conceptual, confrontational, or niche work may be technically excellent but wrong for the Wallspace context.
- **Professional presentation.** Artist has a basic online presence (website, Instagram, or equivalent) and can provide high-resolution images of their work.
- **Venue-appropriate formats.** Artist can produce work in formats suitable for venue display: prints (framed or unframed), canvases, or hangable originals. No sculpture, installation, or video in v1.

### How Many Artists to Launch With

**Target: 30-50 curated artists for launch.**

- Minimum viable: 30 artists (enough to fill 10-15 venues with variety)
- Target: 40-50 artists (provides curation flexibility and rotation depth)
- Maximum at launch: 50 (more than this overwhelms founder-operated curation)

### Categories and Styles to Start With

**Phase 1 (Launch):** Photography only.

Focus on styles that work well in hospitality environments:
- Street photography
- Architectural photography
- Landscape and nature
- Abstract and textural
- Urban and cityscape
- Black and white
- Food and still life (natural fit for cafes/restaurants)
- Travel and documentary

**Phase 2 (Month 3-6):** Add illustration and printmaking.

- Illustration (hand-drawn, digital illustration with original character)
- Printmaking (screen prints, linocuts, risograph)
- Graphic art

**Phase 3 (Month 6-12):** Add painting and mixed media.

- Painting (abstract, contemporary, figurative)
- Mixed media (collage, textile-based)
- Limited edition prints of originals

### Photographers First, Then Artists

Photographers are the right starting segment for three reasons:

1. **Operational simplicity.** Photographs are reproduced as prints. Standardised sizes, consistent production process, easy to frame, easy to ship, easy to replace if damaged.
2. **Lower price points.** Photographic prints typically sell at £80-£300, which is accessible impulse-purchase territory for cafe customers. Original paintings often start at £500+, which is a harder sell in a casual venue setting.
3. **Larger addressable pool.** There are more professional and semi-professional photographers in London than painters. The applicant pool will be deeper.

### Prints, Framed Prints, Originals, or All?

**V1: Focus on prints and framed prints.**

- Unframed prints (artist or Wallspace handles production)
- Framed prints (Wallspace can offer framing as add-on service in v2)
- Originals accepted but not required

Prints are operationally simpler, replaceable if damaged, and enable editions (one image can be placed in multiple venues simultaneously). Originals add value and price diversity but introduce complexity (insurance, damage risk, one-placement-at-a-time constraint).

**Rule:** Every artist must be able to supply work as prints. Originals are welcome as supplementary inventory.

### Exclusivity

**No exclusivity required.**

Artists can sell through other channels (their website, galleries, markets, online platforms). Wallspace does not demand exclusivity.

However:
- Work placed in a Wallspace venue must be available for purchase through Wallspace during its placement period.
- Artists should not undercut Wallspace pricing on other channels (price parity clause).
- If an artist sells a piece directly to someone who discovered it in a Wallspace venue (bypassing the platform), this is a terms violation. Repeat offences result in removal.

### Founding Artist Tier (£29/mo) -- Limited and Permanent

- Available to the first 50 artists only
- Price locked for life (as long as membership remains active)
- Cannot be re-acquired once cancelled
- Designed to reward early adopters and build the initial roster
- Includes all Core tier features except multi-venue placement and priority curation
- Founding artists get a "Founding Artist" badge on their profile

**Strategic purpose:** The Founding tier creates urgency ("only 50 spots"), rewards early risk-takers, and builds a loyal base of artists who have a vested interest in Wallspace succeeding.

### What Each Tier Should and Should Not Promise

**Promise:**
- Guaranteed placement in at least one venue (within 30 days of onboarding completion)
- Professional presentation (proper hanging, labelling, QR codes)
- Working sales infrastructure (online purchase capability, venue QR codes)
- Regular rotation per tier schedule
- Transparent reporting on placement status and any sales

**Do not promise:**
- Sales. Never guarantee sales volume or revenue. Wallspace provides placement and infrastructure; sales depend on the work, pricing, and market.
- Specific venues. Artists can express preferences, but Wallspace makes the curation decision. Do not promise placement in a specific venue.
- Foot traffic numbers. Venue foot traffic varies. Do not guarantee exposure metrics.
- Return on investment. Never frame membership as "you'll make your money back." Frame it as a professional distribution channel with commercial upside.

### How to Make Membership Valuable and Fair

Value must be tangible and visible every month:

1. **Placement confirmation within 30 days.** The single most important deliverable. Artists are paying for their work to be on a wall in a real venue. Deliver this fast.
2. **Monthly status update.** Even if nothing has sold, send a monthly email: where the work is placed, how long until next rotation, any enquiries received, venue foot traffic estimate.
3. **Professional photography of installed work.** Artists can use these images for their own portfolio, social media, and CV. This alone is worth the membership fee to many artists.
4. **Social media visibility.** Feature artists and their installed work on Wallspace social channels. Tag artists, tag venues. This gives artists content and exposure they would otherwise have to create themselves.
5. **Clear, simple sales process.** When a sale happens, the artist gets paid promptly (within 14 days). No chasing invoices, no ambiguity about commission.

### How to Reduce Churn

Artist churn is the primary business risk. Mitigation strategies:

1. **Annual plans with discount.** 15% discount for annual commitment. This reduces monthly churn to annual renewal decisions, which are easier to manage.
2. **Deliver placement fast.** The first 30 days determine whether an artist stays. Get work on a wall within that window.
3. **Non-sales value.** Even before a sale happens, deliver professional photos, social media features, and venue placement proof. Artists who feel seen and professionally represented churn less than artists waiting silently for a sale.
4. **Community (light touch).** A private channel (WhatsApp group or similar) for Wallspace artists. Share venue news, sales celebrations, rotation updates. Keep it low-noise and professional.
5. **Honest communication.** If sales are slow, say so. Explain what Wallspace is doing to improve. Artists respect transparency more than silence.
6. **Exit interviews.** When an artist cancels, ask why. Use the data to fix problems.
7. **Win-back offers.** Artists who cancel can be offered a one-month free return trial after 3+ months away.

### How to Make Artists Feel This Is a Professional Commercial Channel

- **Use professional language.** "Placement," "commission," "portfolio," "catalogue." Not "upload your art" or "share your creativity."
- **Structured onboarding.** Agreement, portfolio review, placement briefing. Not a casual sign-up.
- **Prompt payment.** Net-14 payment terms on sales. Professional invoice/statement provided.
- **Quality standards.** The fact that Wallspace rejects applicants signals that acceptance means something.
- **Venue quality.** Place work in venues that are themselves well-run, attractive, and well-regarded. If the venues look amateurish, the channel feels amateurish.
- **No vanity metrics.** Do not report "impressions" or "likes." Report placements, enquiries, and sales. These are the metrics professionals care about.

## Artist Onboarding Flow

**Step 1: Application (Day 0)**
- Artist submits application via Wallspace website
- Provides: name, bio, website/Instagram, medium, style description
- Uploads: 10-20 representative works (high-resolution images)
- Selects: preferred membership tier
- Answers: pricing expectations, availability, any venue preferences

**Step 2: Review (Days 1-5)**
- Founder reviews portfolio against selection criteria
- Checks online presence for consistency and professionalism
- Decision: Accept, Waitlist, or Decline
- All applicants receive a response within 5 business days

**Step 3: Acceptance and Agreement (Days 5-7)**
- Accepted artists receive welcome email with next steps
- Digital membership agreement sent for signature
- Payment setup (Stripe, monthly or annual)
- First payment collected

**Step 4: Portfolio Preparation (Days 7-14)**
- Artist selects 5-10 works for initial venue placement
- Provides high-resolution files, titles, descriptions, pricing for each
- Wallspace reviews and confirms suitability for venue placement
- Discussion of print specifications, framing preferences, pricing strategy

**Step 5: Placement Matching (Days 14-21)**
- Wallspace matches artist to venue(s) based on style, medium, venue aesthetic, and availability
- Artist notified of placement match with venue details
- Artist confirms or discusses alternatives

**Step 6: Installation (Days 21-30)**
- Work produced (printed, framed if applicable)
- Installation scheduled with venue
- Work installed with labels, QR codes, and pricing
- Artist notified with photos of installed work

**Step 7: Live (Day 30+)**
- Work is live in venue
- Sales infrastructure active (QR codes, online listing)
- Artist receives monthly status updates
- Rotation scheduled per tier

## Artist Selection Criteria

Scoring framework for application review (internal use):

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Technical quality | 30% | Professional execution, clean post-processing (photo), skilled technique (art), print-ready files |
| Commercial viability | 25% | Would this sell in a cafe/restaurant setting? Is it appealing to a broad audience? Appropriate price points? |
| Coherent style | 20% | Recognisable aesthetic, consistent body of work, clear artistic voice |
| Professional presentation | 15% | Clean portfolio, reasonable bio, functioning website/social media |
| Venue fit | 10% | Does the work suit the types of venues Wallspace serves? Scale, subject matter, palette? |

**Automatic decline triggers:**
- Fewer than 10 works submitted
- Obvious AI-generated work
- Work that is offensive, graphic, or clearly inappropriate for hospitality settings
- No online presence whatsoever
- Retail prices wildly out of market (e.g., £5,000 for an unframed print from an unknown photographer)

## Artist Agreement Outline

The membership agreement covers:

1. **Parties.** Wallspace Ltd and the Artist.
2. **Membership tier and fees.** Monthly/annual amount, payment schedule, cancellation terms.
3. **Services provided.** What Wallspace delivers (placement, curation, sales infrastructure, rotation).
4. **Commission structure.** 20% customer sales, 15% venue purchases (18%/13% for Premium tier). Commission applies to all sales facilitated through Wallspace.
5. **Payment terms.** Artist paid within 14 days of confirmed sale. Payment via bank transfer.
6. **Intellectual property.** Artist retains full ownership of all work. Artist grants Wallspace a non-exclusive licence to display, photograph, and promote the work for marketing and sales purposes.
7. **Originality declaration.** Artist warrants all submitted work is original and does not infringe third-party rights.
8. **Quality standards.** Wallspace reserves the right to decline specific works for placement. Membership does not guarantee placement of every submitted piece.
9. **Pricing.** Artist sets retail prices. Wallspace may advise on pricing strategy but does not unilaterally change prices. Price parity clause: artist will not sell the same work for less through other channels.
10. **Damage and loss.** Wallspace carries basic liability insurance for displayed work. Artist is responsible for insuring originals above £500 in value. Wallspace liability capped at production cost for prints.
11. **Exclusivity.** Non-exclusive. Artist may sell through other channels. Work on display in a Wallspace venue must be available for purchase through Wallspace during placement.
12. **Cancellation.** Monthly: 30 days notice. Annual: non-refundable but artist retains placement until end of paid period. Wallspace may terminate with 30 days notice for breach of terms.
13. **Dispute resolution.** Informal resolution first, then mediation, then courts of England and Wales.
14. **Data and privacy.** GDPR-compliant. Artist data used only for service delivery and marketing.

## Artist FAQ

**1. What is Wallspace?**
Wallspace is a curated commercial channel that places professional photography and art into independent venues across London. We handle curation, installation, rotation, and sales. Artists pay a monthly membership for guaranteed placement in high-footfall venues.

**2. How much does membership cost?**
Three tiers: Founding (£29/mo, limited to first 50 artists), Core (£49/mo), and Premium (£89/mo). Annual plans are available at a 15% discount. Each tier offers different levels of placement, promotion, and support.

**3. What does my membership include?**
Guaranteed placement in at least one venue (within 30 days), professional installation with labels and QR codes, online sales listing, regular rotation, monthly status updates, and sales support. Higher tiers include more venues, more frequent rotation, professional photography, and social media promotion.

**4. Will my work definitely sell?**
No. Wallspace provides placement, visibility, and sales infrastructure. We cannot guarantee sales. However, your work will be in front of real people in real spaces every day, which is more exposure than most online platforms provide. Many of our artists see their first sale within the first three months.

**5. How is Wallspace different from a gallery?**
Galleries take 50% commission and are extremely selective. Wallspace takes 15-20% commission and places your work in everyday commercial spaces with higher foot traffic than most galleries. You also retain full pricing control and intellectual property rights.

**6. What commission does Wallspace take?**
20% on customer purchases (someone discovers your work in a venue and buys it) and 15% on direct venue purchases (the venue buys your work for permanent display). Premium tier artists receive reduced rates of 18% and 13% respectively.

**7. How do I get paid when something sells?**
Within 14 days of a confirmed sale, via bank transfer. You receive a clear statement showing the sale price, commission deducted, and net payment. No invoicing required on your end.

**8. Can I choose which venue my work goes to?**
You can express preferences and exclusions, but Wallspace makes the final curation decision. Our matching is based on your style, the venue's aesthetic, and their customer demographic. We aim to place your work where it has the best chance of being appreciated and sold.

**9. What types of work does Wallspace accept?**
Currently: photography (all genres), illustration, printmaking, painting, and mixed media. Work must be hangable (no sculpture, installation, or video). All work must be original -- AI-generated work is not accepted.

**10. Do I need to provide framed work?**
Not necessarily. You can supply unframed prints and we can arrange framing (framing service available at discounted rates for members). If you prefer to frame your own work, that is also fine. We will discuss specifications during onboarding.

**11. Who handles installation and removal?**
Wallspace handles all installation and removal. You do not need to visit the venue or do any physical work. We will send you photos of your installed work.

**12. What happens if my work is damaged in a venue?**
Wallspace carries basic liability insurance for displayed work. For prints, we cover the production cost of replacement. For originals valued above £500, we recommend you arrange your own insurance. Damage is rare but we take it seriously and work with venues to minimise risk.

**13. Is membership exclusive? Can I sell through other channels?**
Membership is non-exclusive. You can continue selling through your website, galleries, markets, and other platforms. The only requirement is that work displayed in a Wallspace venue must be available for purchase through Wallspace during its placement, and you must not undercut Wallspace pricing on other channels.

**14. How do I cancel my membership?**
Monthly members: 30 days written notice. Your work will be removed at the next rotation date. Annual members: non-refundable, but you retain placement until the end of your paid period. Founding tier pricing is locked for life but cannot be re-acquired once cancelled.

**15. How selective is Wallspace? Will I be accepted?**
We review every application against four criteria: technical quality, commercial viability, coherent style, and professional presentation. We are not looking for gallery-level prestige, but we do require professional-standard work that is suitable for venue display. We accept roughly half of applicants. If you are declined, we will provide brief feedback on why.

**16. Can I update or swap the works on display?**
Yes, within your rotation schedule. If you want to swap works outside your scheduled rotation, additional rotation fees may apply (tier-dependent). Contact us to discuss.

**17. What sizes and formats work best?**
For cafe and restaurant settings, medium-format works perform best: A3 to A1 (approximately 30x40cm to 60x84cm). Very small works get lost on large walls; very large works can overwhelm smaller venues. We will advise on sizing during onboarding based on your matched venue.

**18. How does the QR code system work?**
Each displayed work has a discreet QR code and small label with the title, artist name, and price. Scanning the code takes the viewer to the work's online listing where they can purchase, enquire, or browse your other work. The QR code also links to your Wallspace artist profile.

**19. Do venues take a cut of sales?**
In the standard model, no. The venue provides the wall space for free and benefits from improved aesthetics. Wallspace may introduce venue referral bonuses in the future, but these come from Wallspace's commission, not the artist's share.

**20. What if I want to apply but I am not ready to pay immediately?**
You can apply and be reviewed without committing to payment. If accepted, your place is held for 14 days. After that, you would need to reapply. We recommend applying when you are ready to commit, as Founding tier spots are limited.
