# Section 5: Venue Model Strategy

## Core Principle: Venues Pay Nothing

Wallspace is free for venues. Not freemium. Not "free trial." Free.

This is not a temporary launch tactic. It is the structural foundation of the business model. Artists pay for access to venues. Venues are the distribution channel. You do not charge your distribution channel; you make it as easy as possible for them to participate.

---

## Why Free Matters

### 1. Venues are the scarce asset at launch

Wallspace needs venues more than venues need Wallspace. Artists are looking for exposure and sales channels. Venues are busy running a hospitality business and art is a nice-to-have. Any friction -- cost, commitment, complexity -- gives them a reason to say no.

### 2. The economics work without venue fees

Revenue comes from artist memberships (GBP 29-89/month) and sales commissions (15-20%). Venues generate value by providing display space and foot traffic, not by paying fees. Charging venues would shrink the network and reduce the value delivered to paying artists.

### 3. Free removes the decision-maker problem

A cafe owner can say yes to "we'll put art on your walls for free" without consulting a business partner, accountant, or landlord. The moment money is involved, the decision escalates, slows down, or dies.

### 4. Free is the competitive moat

Any future competitor who tries to charge venues will struggle to pull venues away from a free service that already works. Network effects compound: more venues attract more artists, which fund better service, which attracts more venues.

### 5. Free creates word-of-mouth

Venue owners talk to each other. "Wallspace put art on my walls, it looks amazing, and it cost me nothing" is a story that spreads. "I'm paying GBP X/month for an art service" is not.

## What the Venue Gets for Free

### Core Service (Always Free)

| What they get | Details |
|---|---|
| **Curated artwork for their walls** | Hand-selected by Wallspace to suit the venue's aesthetic, space, and clientele |
| **Professional installation** | Wallspace coordinates delivery and hanging. Venue does not need to do anything except approve the selection. |
| **Regular rotation** | Fresh work on a quarterly cycle (or as agreed). Keeps the space feeling alive. |
| **Artist matching** | Wallspace selects artists whose style fits the venue. No browsing, no decision fatigue. |
| **Sales facilitation** | If a customer wants to buy a piece, Wallspace handles the transaction. Venue is not involved in payment processing. |
| **Revenue share on sales** | Venue earns a share of any sale made from their wall. Money for doing nothing beyond displaying work. |
| **Removal and replacement** | If a piece sells, Wallspace replaces it promptly. If a piece is not working, Wallspace swaps it out. |
| **No commitment or contract** | Venue can pause or stop at any time. No lock-in. No penalty. |
| **Small signage / QR cards** | Discreet labels next to each piece with artist name, title, price, and QR code linking to the Wallspace listing. |
| **A better-looking space** | The most basic value: the venue looks better with real art on the walls than with bare walls or mass-produced prints. |

### What the Venue Should NOT Get for Free

These are potential premium add-ons for later. They are excluded from the core free service to preserve margin and create upsell opportunities.

| Excluded from free | Why |
|---|---|
| **Custom commissions** | Bespoke artwork for a specific space costs artist time and Wallspace coordination. This is a premium service. |
| **Interior design consultation** | Advising on wall colours, lighting, layout beyond basic art placement. This is a different service. |
| **Event curation** | Hosting a private view or art event at the venue. Valuable but resource-intensive. |
| **Branded / corporate art programmes** | Art as part of brand identity (e.g., hotel chains wanting a consistent look across locations). Requires strategy work. |
| **Guaranteed artist exclusivity** | "Only we can show this artist" requires contractual complexity and reduces artist placement opportunities. |
| **Insurance for displayed work** | Wallspace covers basic risk in terms (see legal), but full insurance for high-value originals is a premium add-on. |
| **Expedited rotation** | Monthly rotation instead of quarterly. Higher ops cost. |
| **Multi-location coordination** | Managing art across 5+ sites for a hospitality group. Different ops model. |

## Best Venue Types for Launch

### Tier 1: Launch Targets (Months 1-3)

**Independent cafes in East and South London.**

Why:
- Owner-operated. One person makes the decision.
- Walls are often bare or have cheap prints. Clear upgrade.
- Customers linger. They notice and appreciate art. They buy.
- Cafes are social spaces. Art starts conversations. QR codes get scanned.
- Cafe owners are culturally aligned. They care about aesthetics and community.
- Low commercial sensitivity. Art is a nice-to-have, not a business risk.
- Easy to install. Walls are accessible. Opening hours are predictable.

Target: 10-15 cafes in the pilot.

### Tier 2: Early Expansion (Months 3-6)

**Independent restaurants (casual dining), wine bars, and brunch spots.**

Why:
- Similar dynamics to cafes but slightly higher footfall and price point.
- Customers are more likely to buy art in a "lifestyle" dining environment.
- Slightly more complex ops (evening service, busier walls, manager vs. owner).

### Tier 3: Later (Months 6+)

Salons, boutique hotels, coworking spaces. See Section 6 for full segmentation.

## Making the Proposition Irresistible

### The pitch in one sentence

"We put beautiful, original artwork on your walls for free, handle everything, and if anything sells, you earn a share."

### What makes it irresistible

1. **Zero cost.** Not hidden cost. Not "free but..." Actually free.
2. **Zero effort.** Wallspace selects, delivers, installs, and maintains. The venue approves and unlocks the door.
3. **Zero risk.** No contract. No commitment. No penalty for stopping. If the art does not work, Wallspace swaps it. If the venue wants to stop, they stop.
4. **Revenue upside.** Every sale from the wall puts money in the venue's pocket. Passive income from wall space that was previously earning nothing.
5. **Better space.** The venue looks more interesting, more curated, more intentional. This attracts and retains customers.
6. **Cultural credibility.** Supporting local artists is good for brand and community. Venues can legitimately say they support the local art scene.
7. **Conversation starter.** Art gives staff something to talk about with customers. It creates atmosphere and identity.

## Minimising Friction and Perceived Risk

### Friction points and solutions

| Friction | Solution |
|---|---|
| "I need to check with my business partner" | Emphasise: no cost, no contract. This is lower-stakes than choosing a coffee supplier. Offer to send a one-page summary they can forward. |
| "I don't want holes in my walls" | Use picture rail systems, adhesive hooks, or existing fixtures. Offer to survey the space first. Repair any damage on removal. |
| "What if I don't like the art?" | Venue approves all selections before installation. If they do not like it after hanging, Wallspace swaps it within a week. |
| "What if something gets damaged?" | Work displayed in venues is insured/covered by Wallspace up to a reasonable value. The venue is not liable for accidental damage in normal circumstances. Detail this in the venue agreement. |
| "I don't want to deal with customers asking about the art" | Each piece has a small card/QR code. Customers scan and buy directly through Wallspace. Staff do not need to know prices or handle transactions. A simple brief card is provided for staff. |
| "I'm too busy to deal with this" | One 15-minute site visit. One approval message. Wallspace does everything else. |
| "What if I'm renovating / closing for a period?" | Wallspace collects the work and returns it when the venue reopens. No penalty. |
| "I don't want my space to feel like a gallery" | Wallspace curates for the venue's aesthetic. Subtle signage. The art enhances the space; it does not dominate it. |

## Making Participation Operationally Easy

### The venue journey (step by step)

1. **Introduction.** Wallspace contacts the venue (or venue contacts Wallspace). Brief conversation about the concept. 5 minutes.
2. **Site visit.** Wallspace visits the venue, photographs walls, assesses lighting and style, discusses preferences. 15-20 minutes.
3. **Curation.** Wallspace selects 3-8 pieces suited to the space and sends a preview deck (photos, dimensions, style notes) for approval. Venue reviews on their phone. 5 minutes.
4. **Approval.** Venue says yes (or requests swaps). One message.
5. **Installation.** Wallspace arranges delivery and hanging at a time that suits the venue (before opening or during quiet hours). Venue unlocks the door. 30-60 minutes.
6. **Live.** Art is on the walls. QR cards are placed. Staff are given a one-page brief. Done.
7. **Ongoing.** Wallspace checks in monthly. Handles any sales. Rotates work quarterly (or as agreed). Venue does nothing unless they want changes.

### Venue time commitment

- Initial: 30 minutes total (call + site visit + approval).
- Ongoing: Near zero. Occasional message to approve a rotation or confirm a sale pickup.

## Venue FAQ

### General

**1. What is Wallspace?**
Wallspace is a service that puts curated artwork by local artists on the walls of cafes, restaurants, and other venues -- completely free. We handle selection, delivery, installation, and sales. You get better-looking walls and a share of any sales.

**2. How much does it cost?**
Nothing. The service is free for venues. We are funded by artist memberships and sales commissions.

**3. Is there a contract?**
No binding contract. We use a simple participation agreement that either side can end with two weeks notice. No penalties, no lock-in.

**4. Who chooses the art?**
Wallspace curates a selection based on your space, your style, and your preferences. You approve everything before it goes up. If you do not like something, we swap it.

**5. What kind of art is it?**
Primarily photography and prints at launch, expanding to paintings, illustrations, and mixed media. All work is by real, London-based artists.

### Logistics

**6. Who installs the art?**
Wallspace coordinates delivery and installation. We arrange a time that works for you (early morning, quiet afternoon, or closed day). The process takes 30-60 minutes depending on the number of pieces.

**7. Will you damage my walls?**
We use the least invasive hanging method possible (picture rails, adhesive systems, or small fixings). If any repair is needed when we remove work, we handle it.

**8. How often does the art change?**
Typically every 3 months. If a piece sells, we replace it promptly. You can request changes at any time.

**9. What happens if a piece is damaged?**
Work displayed through Wallspace is covered for accidental damage in normal venue conditions up to GBP 500 per piece. You are not liable for normal wear and tear or accidental damage. Deliberate damage or gross negligence is excluded.

**10. What do my staff need to do?**
Almost nothing. Each piece has a small card with artist name, title, and QR code. If a customer asks about the art, staff can point them to the card or the QR code. We provide a simple one-page brief for staff.

### Sales

**11. How do sales work?**
A customer sees a piece they like, scans the QR code, and purchases through Wallspace. We handle payment, coordinate collection/delivery, and replace the piece on the wall.

**12. Do I earn anything from sales?**
Yes. You receive 10% of the sale price for any piece sold from your venue. This is passive income -- you do not need to do anything beyond having the work on display.

**13. Who handles the money?**
Wallspace processes all payments. You receive your share monthly via bank transfer.

**14. Can I buy the art myself?**
Yes. Venue owners and staff can purchase displayed work. You receive a 10% venue discount instead of the revenue share in that case.

### Concerns

**15. What if I do not like the art you suggest?**
You approve everything before installation. We typically present 2-3 options per wall space. If nothing works, we go back and find alternatives. Your taste matters -- we are curating for your space.

**16. Will this make my cafe feel like a gallery?**
No. We curate to enhance your existing aesthetic, not override it. Signage is small and discreet. The art should feel like a natural part of your space.

**17. What if I want to stop?**
Give us two weeks notice. We collect the work and repair any wall fixings. No questions, no penalty.

**18. Is this a trial or is it always free?**
The core service is designed to be permanently free for venues. Our business model does not depend on charging you.

**19. What about my landlord? Do I need permission?**
Most commercial leases allow tenants to hang items on walls. If you are unsure, we can provide a brief letter explaining the service for your landlord. We use non-invasive hanging methods wherever possible.

**20. Do you work with chains or only independents?**
At launch, we focus on independent venues. We plan to work with small groups and chains later, with a tailored service.

## Venue Objections and Answers

### "I already have art on my walls"

*Response:* That is great -- you clearly value how your space looks. Is the current art getting the attention it deserves? Our artists' work comes with QR codes so customers can learn about the artist and buy pieces they love. We can work alongside what you have, or offer a fresh rotation that keeps things interesting.

### "My walls are not suitable"

*Response:* We would love to take a look. We work with all kinds of spaces -- small walls, awkward corners, exposed brick, picture rails. Even 2-3 well-placed pieces can transform a room. A quick 10-minute visit will tell us what is possible.

### "I'm worried about theft or damage"

*Response:* Displayed work is covered by Wallspace for accidental damage up to GBP 500 per piece. We use secure hanging systems. In practice, theft and damage in venue settings are extremely rare -- art on a cafe wall is not a high-theft target.

### "I don't have time for this"

*Response:* The whole process takes about 30 minutes of your time: a short call, a quick site visit, and an approval message. After that, we handle everything. Most venue owners tell us it is the lowest-effort improvement they have made to their space.

### "What's the catch?"

*Response:* There is no catch. We are funded by artists who pay a membership to access our venue network. Venues are free because you are providing something valuable: wall space and foot traffic. It is a fair exchange.

### "I need to ask my business partner / landlord"

*Response:* Completely understand. We can send you a one-page summary to share with them. Happy to jump on a quick call with them too. Since there is no cost or binding contract, most partners are comfortable once they understand the proposition.

### "I tried something like this before and it didn't work"

*Response:* We hear that. A lot of art-in-venues schemes are poorly organised -- random artists dropping off work, no curation, no support. Wallspace is a managed service. We curate for your space, handle all logistics, and check in regularly. If anything is not working, we fix it.

### "I don't think my customers care about art"

*Response:* You might be surprised. Research consistently shows that customers spend more time (and more money) in spaces that feel thoughtfully designed. Art is one of the easiest ways to create that feeling. And if a customer does buy a piece, you earn a share of the sale.

### "What if the art puts off my customers?"

*Response:* We curate specifically for your venue and your clientele. We do not put provocative or polarising work in a family-friendly brunch spot. You approve every piece before it goes up, and we swap anything that is not working.

### "We're a small venue -- is it worth it for just a couple of pieces?"

*Response:* Absolutely. Even 2-3 well-chosen pieces can change the feel of a room. Some of our best venue partnerships are compact spaces where every piece gets noticed.

### "Do I need to promote the art?"

*Response:* Not at all. The QR cards do the work. But if you want to share it on your social media, we provide images and copy you can use. It is good content -- your followers will engage with it.

### "Can I choose the specific pieces?"

*Response:* We present a curated shortlist based on your space and style preferences. You choose from that shortlist. We find this works better than open browsing because our team knows which pieces work well in venue settings.
