# Section 11: Demand-Side Strategy

## Purpose

This section defines how Wallspace recruits and retains venues. Venues are the scarce resource in this marketplace: without great venues, artists see no value. Venues must be acquired through founder-led, high-touch outreach, not marketing funnels. The first 10-15 venues determine whether Wallspace has a viable business.

---

## 11.1 Strategic Principles

1. **Venues pay nothing.** The core Wallspace service is always free for venues. This removes the biggest objection and makes the pitch a pure value-add conversation.
2. **Start narrow.** Independent cafes and restaurants in East and South London are the launch segment. Not chains, not offices, not retail -- not yet.
3. **Founder-led sales only.** No sales hires at launch. The founder does every pitch, every installation, every follow-up. This builds deep understanding of venue needs and objections.
4. **Win through service, not features.** The pitch is not "use our platform." The pitch is "we will handle everything for you -- selection, delivery, installation, rotation, and sales."
5. **Case studies are the growth engine.** Every successful placement becomes a story that sells the next 5 venues. Photograph everything. Get testimonials early.

---

## 11.2 Best First Niche: Independent Cafes in East and South London

### Why Independent Cafes

- **Aesthetic awareness.** Indie cafe owners already care about interior design and atmosphere. They spend money on decor. Artwork is a natural extension.
- **Blank wall space.** Most indie cafes have large walls that are either bare or displaying mediocre art. The opportunity is visible.
- **Customer dwell time.** Cafe customers sit for 20-60 minutes. They look at walls. They notice art. This drives engagement and sales.
- **Owner-operator decision-making.** One person makes the decision. No corporate approval chains, procurement departments, or brand guidelines.
- **Social proof amplification.** Cafe owners talk to each other. A successful placement in one cafe generates word-of-mouth to 5-10 nearby venues.
- **Instagram culture.** Indie cafes already photograph their spaces and post to Instagram. Artwork in the background becomes organic marketing.

### Why East and South London

- **Density of independent cafes.** Shoreditch, Hackney, Dalston, Peckham, Brixton, Deptford, Bermondsey, Camberwell -- these areas have high concentrations of design-conscious independent hospitality venues.
- **Creative community overlap.** Many of the target artists live and work in these areas. Local connections are powerful.
- **Geographic clustering enables efficient logistics.** Installation, rotation, and check-ins are faster when venues are within a few miles of each other.
- **Cultural credibility.** Launching in these areas establishes Wallspace as creatively credible. Expanding to other London zones (or other cities) from this base is easier than starting in a less culturally resonant area.

### Target Neighbourhoods (Priority Order)

1. Peckham / Camberwell / Denmark Hill
2. Bermondsey / London Bridge
3. Hackney / Dalston / London Fields
4. Shoreditch / Bethnal Green
5. Deptford / New Cross
6. Brixton / Herne Hill
7. Stoke Newington / Clapton

## 11.3 First 100 Venue Target Strategy

### How to Identify Targets

**Method 1: Walk the Streets (Highest Quality)**
- Walk every target neighbourhood systematically. Enter every independent cafe and restaurant.
- Assess: wall space available, current decor quality, vibe alignment, customer type, owner accessibility.
- Take notes and photos (discreetly). Record venue name, address, Instagram handle, owner name if visible.
- Target: 5-8 venues per walk, 3-4 walks per week in weeks 1-4.

**Method 2: Google Maps and Instagram Scouting**
- Search Google Maps for independent cafes in each target neighbourhood.
- Filter by rating (4.0+), review count (50+), and photos showing interior.
- Cross-reference on Instagram. Look for venues that post about their space, not just their food.
- Add to prospect list with notes on interior, apparent wall space, aesthetic alignment.
- Target: Build a list of 100 venues in the first 2 weeks.

**Method 3: Existing Knowledge and Networks**
- Personal favourite cafes and restaurants in the target areas.
- Recommendations from friends, artists, and early supporters.
- Venues featured in Time Out, Eater London, Infatuation for design/atmosphere.

**Method 4: Event-Based Discovery**
- Venues that host events (live music, poetry, talks) are more likely to be open to art.
- Check Eventbrite, Dice, DesignMyNight for venue-based events in target areas.

### Prospect List Management

Maintain a simple Airtable or Notion database with fields:
- Venue name
- Address / neighbourhood
- Instagram handle
- Contact person (name, role)
- Contact method (email, DM, in-person)
- Wall space assessment (small / medium / large)
- Aesthetic alignment (1-5)
- Current decor status (bare walls / mediocre art / good art already)
- Outreach status (not contacted / contacted / interested / meeting scheduled / onboarded / declined)
- Notes and follow-up dates

## 11.4 Founder-Led Sales Strategy

### The Core Motion

The founder's job in months 1-3 is to spend 60-70% of working time on venue acquisition. This means:

- **3-4 neighbourhood walks per week** (2-3 hours each). Enter venues, have conversations, leave cards.
- **10-15 cold emails or DMs per week** to pre-scouted venues.
- **2-3 in-person meetings per week** with interested venue owners.
- **1-2 installations per week** once first venues are confirmed.

### Sales Cycle

| Stage | Timeline | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Identify | Ongoing | Walk streets, scout online, get referrals |
| First touch | Day 1 | Cold email, DM, or in-person visit |
| Follow-up | Day 4-5 | Second email or visit if no response |
| Meeting | Day 7-14 | 15-20 min in-person conversation at the venue |
| Proposal | Same day or day after meeting | Send 3-5 curated artwork options tailored to their space |
| Decision | Day 14-21 | Venue confirms which pieces they want |
| Installation | Within 7 days of confirmation | Founder delivers and installs artwork |
| Check-in | Day 14 post-installation | Visit or call to see how it is going |

Average time from first touch to installation: 2-4 weeks.

- Lead with what they get, not what Wallspace is.

## 11.6 Walk-In Strategy

### Preparation

- Visit during quiet periods (typically 2-4pm for cafes, 10-11am for restaurants).
- Bring physical samples: 2-3 high-quality photographic prints (A3 or larger) to show, not to leave.
- Carry a simple one-page leave-behind card (not a brochure -- a card with the key proposition and a QR code to the venue landing page).
- Know the venue's Instagram before walking in. Reference specific things about their space.

### In-Person Pitch Script

**Opening (at the counter or with the owner):**

> "Hi, are you the owner? [If not: Is the owner around today?]"

**If owner is available:**

> "Hi [Name if known], I'm [founder name] from Wallspace. We work with independent venues like yours to put curated artwork on your walls -- photography, prints, original pieces. It's completely free for you and we handle everything: selection, delivery, installation, rotation. I was walking past and thought your space would be perfect for it. Do you have two minutes?"

**If they say yes (the 2-minute version):**

> "So basically, we have a roster of about 40-50 curated photographers and artists. I'd come in, look at your walls, and put together a shortlist of 3-5 pieces that match your space and vibe. We deliver and install everything. The artwork rotates every 6-8 weeks so your space always feels fresh.
>
> Your customers can buy any piece they see -- there's a small QR code next to each one. If something sells, you get a commission -- usually around 15-20% of the sale price. And it genuinely makes the space feel more interesting for your customers.
>
> We're working with [venue name] down the road and they've had a great response. Would you be open to trying it with a couple of pieces? Absolutely no commitment -- if you don't like it after a month, we just come and take them down."

**If they ask about cost:**

> "Nothing. It's completely free for you. We make our money from a small membership that artists pay to be on our roster, and a cut of sales. You never pay a thing."

**If they say they're busy / not now:**

> "Totally understand. Can I leave you this card? It's got everything on it. And I'll send you a quick email with some examples of what we've done in other spaces. What's the best email for you?"

**If they say no:**

> "No problem at all. If you ever change your mind, there's my details. Enjoy the rest of your day."

**If they're interested but want to think about it:**

> "Of course. How about I send you a few photos of pieces that would work in your space? No commitment at all -- just so you can see what it would look like. What's the best email?"

### Post-Visit Actions

- Add to prospect database within 1 hour.
- Send follow-up email within 24 hours with 2-3 artwork photos tailored to their space.
- Follow their Instagram account.
- Schedule follow-up in 5 days if no response.

## 11.7 Instagram and LinkedIn Strategy

### Instagram (Primary Social Channel for Venues)

**Goal:** Make Wallspace's Instagram a visual portfolio of "art in spaces" that venue owners want to be part of.

**Content strategy (3-5 posts per week once placements begin):**
1. **Installation shots** -- the artwork on the venue wall, beautifully photographed. Tag venue and artist. (40% of posts)
2. **Before/after shots** -- bare wall vs. wall with artwork. Dramatic and shareable. (15% of posts)
3. **Artist spotlights** -- introduce a roster artist, show 3-4 pieces, explain their practice. (20% of posts)
4. **Venue spotlights** -- feature a partner venue, explain why the space works for art. (15% of posts)
5. **Behind the scenes** -- installation process, curation meetings, artwork selection. (10% of posts)

**Outreach via Instagram:**
- Follow target venues and engage with their posts (genuine comments, not spam).
- After 1-2 weeks of engagement, DM with a personalised version of the pitch.
- Share venue posts to Stories and tag them -- build the relationship before the pitch.

### LinkedIn (Secondary Channel -- Office and Corporate Outreach)

- Not a priority for the cafe/restaurant launch.
- Begin building presence from month 3 in preparation for office/co-working venue expansion.
- Post 1-2 times per week about the intersection of art, design, and commercial spaces.
- Connect with office managers, co-working space operators, interior designers.

## 11.8 Local Partnership Strategy

### Interior Designers

**Approach:** Interior designers working on hospitality fit-outs are potential referral partners. They specify furniture, lighting, and finishes -- artwork is often an afterthought that they would happily outsource.

**How to engage:**
- Identify designers who work on London hospitality projects (search Instagram, Houzz, Design Week).
- Offer a referral arrangement: for every venue they introduce that becomes a Wallspace partner, they receive a commission on sales from that venue (5%) for the first 12 months.
- Position Wallspace as their "art solution" -- they get to offer their clients a complete service without doing the work.
- Target: 3-5 designer relationships in months 2-4.

### Hospitality Suppliers and Consultants

**Approach:** Coffee roasters, food suppliers, POS system providers, and hospitality consultants all have existing relationships with independent cafe owners.

**How to engage:**
- Identify local specialty coffee roasters who supply to indie cafes (e.g., Square Mile, Origin, Assembly, Allpress).
- Propose co-marketing: Wallspace recommends their partner roasters to venues; they mention Wallspace to their cafe clients.
- Attend hospitality trade events and casual meetups where suppliers and owners mix.

### Local Business Networks

- Join local business networks in target neighbourhoods (Peckham Business Network, Hackney Business Network, local chambers of commerce).
- Attend BNI or similar referral groups where hospitality owners participate.
- Offer to do a free "art consultation" for any venue in the network.

## 11.9 Referral Strategy

### Venue-to-Venue Referrals

**Timing:** Activate at month 2-3, once first 5+ venues are live and happy.

**Mechanism:**
- After a positive 2-week check-in, ask: "Do you know any other venue owners in the area who might like this? We'd love an introduction."
- Offer a tangible incentive: the referring venue gets a bonus artwork rotation (free swap of all pieces) or a higher commission rate on sales for 3 months.
- Make it easy: "Just text them my number, or I can send you a short message to forward."

**Target:** Each happy venue should generate 1-2 warm introductions. With 10 live venues, this should produce 10-20 warm leads.

### Artist-to-Venue Referrals

- Artists often know cafe and restaurant owners personally, or frequent venues that would be ideal.
- Ask every artist during onboarding: "Are there any venues where you'd love to see your work displayed? Can you introduce us?"
- No formal incentive needed -- artists benefit directly from more placement opportunities.

## 11.10 Case Study Strategy

### Why Case Studies Are Critical

Case studies convert venues faster than any pitch. A venue owner seeing that a similar venue in their neighbourhood is doing it -- and enjoying it -- is the most powerful sales tool.

### Case Study Format (One Page)

1. **Venue name and location** (with photo of the space)
2. **The situation:** What the walls looked like before. Why they were interested.
3. **What Wallspace did:** How many pieces placed, what style, how installation worked.
4. **The result:** Customer response, any sales, owner's experience.
5. **Owner quote:** 2-3 sentences in their own words.

### Case Study Execution

- Create the first case study within 2 weeks of the first installation.
- Photograph the installation professionally (or at minimum, with a good phone in good light).
- Interview the venue owner for 10 minutes. Record the call (with permission) and pull the best quotes.
- Format as a simple PDF and as a web page on the Wallspace site.
- Use in all outreach: attach to follow-up emails, show on phone during walk-ins, post on Instagram, add to venue landing page.

### Target: 3 Case Studies in First 2 Months

| Case Study | Venue Type | Neighbourhood | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Independent cafe | Peckham or Bermondsey | Prove the core concept |
| 2 | Restaurant | Hackney or Shoreditch | Show versatility beyond cafes |
| 3 | Different style venue | Any target area | Demonstrate range of artwork matching |

## 11.12 Hospitality and Office Outreach Strategy (Month 3+)

### Restaurants (Month 2-3)

- Same approach as cafes but target restaurants with evening service (art is viewed during longer dining experiences).
- Approach during lunch service (less hectic than dinner).
- Adjust art selection: restaurants may prefer larger statement pieces and more dramatic work.

### Co-Working Spaces (Month 4-6)

- Different value proposition: art as a workplace amenity and culture signal.
- Contact office/community managers, not founders.
- Offer monthly rotation to keep the space feeling dynamic.
- Channels: LinkedIn outreach, co-working directories, direct visits.

### Hotels and Boutique Accommodation (Month 6+)

- Longer sales cycle, higher stakes, larger wall count per venue.
- Requires a more polished pitch deck and potentially a sample installation.
- Start with boutique hotels (under 50 rooms) where the owner makes aesthetic decisions.

## 11.13 Referral Loop Design

### The Flywheel

```
Venue signs up
    --> Great artwork installed
        --> Venue owner is happy
            --> Venue owner tells other owners (organic)
            --> Wallspace asks for referral (prompted)
                --> New venue introduced (warm lead)
                    --> Higher conversion rate
                        --> More installations
                            --> More case studies
                                --> Even easier to recruit next venue
```

### Referral Activation Checklist

- [ ] Installation complete and venue owner confirmed happy (2-week check-in)
- [ ] Installation photographed and shared on Instagram (tagging venue)
- [ ] Case study created (even if brief)
- [ ] Referral ask made: "Know anyone else who'd like this?"
- [ ] Incentive offered: bonus rotation or enhanced commission
- [ ] Follow up on any introductions within 48 hours
- [ ] Track referral source in CRM for every new venue

### Referral Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Referrals requested per happy venue | 100% (ask every one) |
| Introductions received per ask | 0.5-1.0 |
| Conversion rate on warm referrals | 30-50% |
| Time from referral to installation | Under 3 weeks |
| Percentage of new venues from referrals (after month 3) | 30-50% |
