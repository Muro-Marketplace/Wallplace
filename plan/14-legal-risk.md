## Part 2: Contract Checklist -- Key Terms by Agreement

### Artist Agreement -- Key Terms

**Relationship:**
- Wallspace acts as agent, not buyer. Artists retain ownership of their works at all times until sold.
- Relationship is consignment/agency, not employment.
- Artist is a member of the Wallspace platform, not an employee or contractor.

**Membership:**
- Tier selected (Founding/Core/Premium) and monthly fee.
- Billing date, payment method, auto-renewal.
- What each tier includes (number of works displayed, curation priority, etc.).
- Cancellation terms: cancel anytime, effective end of billing period.
- 14-day cooling-off period for new members (Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013).
- No refunds after cooling-off period for current billing period.

**Consignment and Display:**
- Artist grants Wallspace the right to display works at approved venues.
- Artist retains full ownership until point of sale.
- Wallspace selects venues in consultation with artist preferences (but final placement at Wallspace discretion).
- Standard loan period: 3 months, renewable.
- Artist can recall works with 30 days written notice.

**Sales and Commission:**
- Customer sale (via venue QR/online): 20% Wallspace commission.
- Venue purchase: 15% Wallspace commission.
- Artist sets retail price; Wallspace may advise but cannot change without consent.
- Payout within 14 days of sale completion (after cooling-off period).
- Payout method: bank transfer or Stripe Connect.

**Insurance and Liability:**
- Wallspace carries transit insurance for works being moved between locations.
- While on display at venue: venue's responsibility per Venue Agreement.
- Artist must declare accurate insurance/replacement values.
- Wallspace liability capped at declared insurance value.

**IP and Image Rights:**
- Artist grants Wallspace a non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to use images of their works for marketing, website, social media, and venue presentation.
- Artist retains all copyright and IP in their works.
- Wallspace will credit the artist in all uses.
- Licence continues for 12 months after artist leaves platform (for archival/portfolio purposes), unless artist requests removal.

**Termination:**
- Either party can terminate with 30 days notice.
- On termination: all works returned within 30 days, final payouts processed, membership cancelled.
- Outstanding sales completed and paid out per normal terms.

### Venue Agreement -- Key Terms

**Service:**
- Wallspace provides curated artwork on free loan for display.
- No cost to venue for core service.
- Artwork refreshed on agreed schedule (default: quarterly).

**Venue Obligations:**
- Provide agreed wall space in good condition.
- Provide or allow installation of appropriate hanging hardware.
- Exercise reasonable care for displayed artwork (same standard as own property).
- Allow Wallspace signage and QR codes adjacent to artwork.
- Not remove, relocate, or alter displayed artwork without Wallspace approval.
- Notify Wallspace within 24 hours of any damage, loss, or theft.
- Allow Wallspace access for installation, removal, and inspection during business hours.

**Liability:**
- Venue responsible for artwork while on display at their premises.
- Venue must maintain public liability insurance (minimum £1m recommended).
- Venue liable for damage caused by venue staff, customers, or conditions within venue's control.
- Damage compensation: repair cost or declared insurance value, whichever is lower.
- Exclusions: force majeure, inherent vice (artwork deteriorating on its own).

**Sales:**
- Venue staff should direct purchase enquiries to QR code / Wallspace.
- Venue may express interest in purchasing displayed works (at venue rate, 15% commission).
- Venue does not collect money on behalf of Wallspace.

**Duration and Termination:**
- Initial period: 3 months.
- Rolling renewal unless either party gives 30 days notice.
- On termination: artwork collected within 14 days, signage removed.

### Customer Terms of Sale

**Consumer Rights:**
- All sales to consumers comply with Consumer Rights Act 2015 and Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013.
- 14-day cooling-off period for distance sales (QR code purchases are distance sales).
- Right to return within 14 days for full refund (artwork must be in original condition).
- Customer pays return shipping unless artwork is faulty or not as described.

**Product Description:**
- Works described accurately: medium, dimensions, condition.
- Photographs are representative but colours may vary slightly due to screen settings.
- Edition information clearly stated (if applicable).

**Delivery:**
- Collection from venue or delivery arranged.
- Delivery timeframe communicated at point of sale.
- Risk passes to customer on delivery.

**Payment:**
- Secure payment via Stripe.
- Prices include VAT where applicable.
- Full payment at point of order.

## Part 4: GDPR and Data Protection

### Data Controller Status
Wallspace is the data controller for all personal data collected through the platform.

### Data Collected

| Data Category | From Whom | Lawful Basis | Retention |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Name, email, phone | Artists | Contract performance | Duration of membership + 6 years |
| Portfolio images, artist statements | Artists | Contract performance | Duration of membership + 12 months |
| Payment details | Artists & Customers | Contract performance | Handled by Stripe (PCI compliant) |
| Name, email, phone | Venue contacts | Legitimate interest | Duration of relationship + 2 years |
| Name, email, address | Customers | Contract performance | 6 years (tax/accounting requirement) |
| Website analytics | All visitors | Consent (cookies) | Per analytics tool retention |
| Marketing preferences | All contacts | Consent | Until consent withdrawn |

### GDPR Requirements Checklist

- [ ] Privacy Policy published on website (clear, plain English)
- [ ] Cookie consent mechanism on website (banner with opt-in)
- [ ] Data processing records maintained (Article 30 register)
- [ ] Subject access request process documented (respond within 30 days)
- [ ] Right to erasure process documented
- [ ] Data breach notification process documented (72-hour ICO notification if applicable)
- [ ] Stripe DPA (Data Processing Agreement) in place
- [ ] Any other processor DPAs in place (email marketing, analytics)
- [ ] Marketing consent collected separately from service consent
- [ ] Unsubscribe mechanism in all marketing emails
- [ ] ICO registration completed (£40/year for small organisations)

### ICO Registration
- Register with the Information Commissioner's Office as a data controller.
- Fee: £40/year for micro-organisations (fewer than 10 staff, turnover under £632k).
- Register at ico.org.uk before processing personal data.

## Part 5: Tax and VAT Overview

### Company Structure
- Wallspace should be a UK limited company (Ltd).
- Register with Companies House and HMRC.

### VAT Registration
- Mandatory when taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 (2024/25 threshold -- check current threshold at launch).
- Consider voluntary registration earlier if:
  - You want to reclaim VAT on business expenses.
  - It adds credibility with venues and corporate clients.
- Note: if artists are not VAT-registered, their prices are VAT-inclusive at their level. Wallspace's commission is a service and would be subject to VAT if Wallspace is VAT-registered.

### Commission and Payout Tax Implications
- Wallspace receives commission income (taxable).
- Artist payouts: Wallspace is not employing artists. Artists are responsible for their own tax.
- Consider whether to use self-billing (Wallspace issues invoices on behalf of artists) -- simpler for artists, requires HMRC agreement and artist consent.
- If using self-billing: maintain self-billing agreement with each artist.

### Membership Income
- Membership fees are standard taxable income for Wallspace.
- If VAT-registered: membership fees are subject to VAT at standard rate (20%).

### Record Keeping
- Keep all financial records for minimum 6 years.
- Use accounting software (Xero or FreeAgent) from Day 1.
- Engage an accountant before first tax year end.

## Part 6: Insurance

### Insurance Wallspace Should Carry

| Policy | Coverage | Estimated Cost | Priority |
|--------|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Public Liability** | Injury/damage during installations | £300-500/year | Essential before first installation |
| **Goods in Transit** | Artwork damage during transport | £200-400/year | Essential before moving artwork |
| **Professional Indemnity** | Claims from bad advice or service failure | £300-600/year | Important -- get within 3 months |
| **Employers' Liability** | Required if hiring staff | £200-400/year | Required when hiring |
| **Cyber/Data** | Data breach costs | £200-400/year | Advisable once processing payments |

### Insurance Venues Should Carry
- Public liability (minimum £1m) -- Wallspace should verify this during onboarding.
- Contents insurance that covers third-party property on premises.
- Note: most commercial venues already have this. Wallspace should request a copy of the venue's insurance certificate.

### Insurance Gap: Artwork on Display
- Standard venue insurance may not explicitly cover consigned artwork.
- Options:
  1. Venue adds consigned artwork to their contents insurance (ideal, low cost for them).
  2. Wallspace takes out a "stock in trade" policy covering all displayed works (more expensive, more control).
  3. Accept the risk at low values and self-insure (viable for works under £500, risky above).
- Recommendation: Start with option 3 for works under £500 and require option 1 for higher-value works. Move to option 2 when portfolio value at venues exceeds £20,000 total.

## Part 7: Installation Liability and Health & Safety

### Risk Areas
- Working at height (hanging artwork above head height).
- Drilling into walls (risk of hitting wiring or pipes).
- Handling heavy or fragile items.
- Working in food-service environments (hygiene, customer safety).

### Mitigation
- Basic risk assessment before each installation.
- Never work at dangerous heights without proper equipment (stepladder maximum -- no climbing on furniture).
- Use stud/pipe detector before drilling.
- Never install during busy service periods.
- Carry public liability insurance.
- If using contractors: ensure they have their own insurance and follow these standards.

### Documentation
- Keep a simple installation risk assessment template.
- Photograph installations for evidence of proper hanging.
- Maintain a log of installations and any incidents.

## Part 8: Authenticity and Editioning

### Risks
- Artist misrepresents edition size (claims limited edition, actually open edition).
- Artist sells "originals" that are reproductions.
- Print quality or medium misrepresented.

### Wallspace Position
- Wallspace does not authenticate artworks. Artist represents and warrants authenticity.
- Artist Agreement must include:
  - Artist warrants they are the creator or rights holder.
  - Artist warrants edition information is accurate.
  - Artist warrants medium and materials description is accurate.
  - Artist indemnifies Wallspace against claims arising from misrepresentation.

### Best Practice
- For photographs/prints: require artists to declare edition size, confirm whether certificate of authenticity is provided, and state print method/paper type.
- For original works: request provenance statement.
- If a customer disputes authenticity: Wallspace facilitates resolution between artist and customer but is not liable.

## Part 9: Risk Register

### Risk Scoring
- **Likelihood:** 1 (Rare) to 5 (Almost Certain)
- **Severity:** 1 (Negligible) to 5 (Critical)
- **Risk Score:** Likelihood x Severity (1-25)
- **Risk Level:** Low (1-6), Medium (7-12), High (13-19), Critical (20-25)

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Severity | Score | Level | Mitigation |
|---|------|-----------|----------|-------|-------|------------|
| 1 | Artwork damaged at venue (minor) | 4 | 2 | 8 | Medium | Condition reports, venue liability clause, insurance for high-value works |
| 2 | Artwork damaged in transit | 3 | 3 | 9 | Medium | Professional packaging, transit insurance, condition reports |
| 3 | Artwork stolen from venue | 2 | 4 | 8 | Medium | Venue security requirements, insurance, avoid very high-value works in low-security venues |
| 4 | Artist disputes commission calculation | 2 | 2 | 4 | Low | Transparent pricing, clear agreement terms, payout breakdown emails |
| 5 | Venue refuses to return artwork | 1 | 4 | 4 | Low | Clear agreement terms, legal recourse, documented ownership |
| 6 | Customer claims artwork not as described | 2 | 3 | 6 | Low | Accurate descriptions, quality photos, consumer rights compliance |
| 7 | Artist leaves with outstanding commitments | 3 | 2 | 6 | Low | 30-day notice period, clear exit process |
| 8 | Data breach / GDPR violation | 1 | 5 | 5 | Low | Minimal data collection, Stripe handles payments, security best practices |
| 9 | Installation causes property damage | 2 | 3 | 6 | Low | Risk assessment, pipe/wire detector, public liability insurance |
| 10 | Payment processor issues (Stripe) | 1 | 4 | 4 | Low | Monitor alerts, backup payment method, maintain reserves |
| 11 | Artist misrepresents work (authenticity) | 1 | 4 | 4 | Low | Artist warranty clause, curation review, indemnity |
| 12 | Venue closes / goes out of business | 3 | 3 | 9 | Medium | Short loan periods, regular check-ins, quick collection process |
| 13 | Founder illness / incapacity | 2 | 5 | 10 | Medium | Document all SOPs, ensure another person can access key systems |
| 14 | Cash flow crunch (membership revenue vs costs) | 3 | 4 | 12 | Medium | Conservative spending, maintain 3-month runway, monitor unit economics |
| 15 | Negative PR / social media incident | 2 | 3 | 6 | Low | Professional conduct, complaint handling process, quick response |
| 16 | Tax/VAT miscalculation | 2 | 3 | 6 | Low | Engage accountant early, use accounting software, set aside tax reserves |
| 17 | Artist IP infringement (submits work they don't own) | 1 | 4 | 4 | Low | Artist warranty, curation review, takedown process |
| 18 | Venue health & safety incident related to artwork | 1 | 5 | 5 | Low | Secure hanging, weight limits, no glass in certain venues, public liability insurance |
| 19 | Multiple artists cancelling simultaneously | 2 | 3 | 6 | Low | Build cohort loyalty, deliver value, maintain pipeline of new artists |
| 20 | Commission model challenged legally | 1 | 4 | 4 | Low | Clear contractual terms, market-standard rates, legal review of agreement |

### Top 5 Risks to Monitor

1. **Cash flow crunch (Score: 12)** -- Most dangerous business risk. Membership revenue must cover operations before commission revenue ramps. Monitor monthly.
2. **Founder incapacity (Score: 10)** -- Single point of failure at launch. Mitigate by documenting everything and having a trusted person who can access systems.
3. **Artwork damage at venue (Score: 8)** -- Most frequent operational risk. Will happen. Process discipline in condition reporting is the defence.
4. **Venue closure (Score: 9)** -- London hospitality industry has high turnover. Quick artwork recovery process is essential.
5. **Artwork damaged in transit (Score: 9)** -- Preventable with proper packaging and handling. Transit insurance is non-negotiable.

## Part 11: Misrepresentation and Platform Risks

### What Could Go Wrong

| Risk | Scenario | Prevention |
|------|----------|------------|
| Artist inflates prices | Lists work at 3x market rate, no sales, blames Wallspace | Price guidance during onboarding; data on comparable sales |
| Artist claims Wallspace damaged work | Work was already damaged; artist tries to claim | Condition reports with photos before every move |
| Venue claims they never agreed to terms | Dispute over agreement contents | Digital signatures, timestamped agreements, email confirmation |
| Customer claims work is different to listing | Colour, size, or condition doesn't match | Accurate descriptions, quality photos, disclaimer about screen variation |
| Artist accuses Wallspace of favouritism | Some artists get better venues | Transparent placement criteria; rotate opportunities |
| Venue uses Wallspace art in their own marketing without credit | Photos of interior shared on social media without artist credit | Address in venue agreement; request tagging |
| Someone copies an artist's work after seeing it at a venue | IP theft | Cannot fully prevent; artist retains IP; note in customer-facing materials |

### Platform Terms to Include
- Wallspace reserves the right to refuse or remove any artist or work at its discretion.
- Wallspace reserves the right to decline any venue.
- Wallspace does not guarantee sales, placement, or income to any artist.
- Membership does not guarantee continuous display of works.
- Wallspace acts as agent, not principal, in sales transactions.
