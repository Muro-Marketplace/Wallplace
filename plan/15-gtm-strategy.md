# Section 15: Go-to-Market Strategy

## Core GTM Positioning

### The One-Line Pitch
**For artists:** "Get your work on the walls of London's best independent venues -- we handle everything."
**For venues:** "Beautiful, rotating artwork for your walls. Completely free. We handle everything."
**For customers:** "Discover and buy original art in your favourite local spots."

### The Narrative
Wallspace exists because thousands of incredible photographs and artworks sit in artists' studios while thousands of bare walls in London's cafes and restaurants could be transformed by them. We make the connection effortless for both sides.

### Why This Angle Works
- **For artists:** Solves the exhibition problem. Most emerging photographers cannot get gallery representation. Wallspace gives them commercial wall space, audience exposure, and sales opportunities without gallery gatekeeping.
- **For venues:** Solves the interior design problem. Most independent cafes know their walls look bare but don't have the budget, time, or taste to fix it. Wallspace does it for free.
- **For the market:** Art should be encountered in daily life, not locked behind gallery doors. Wallspace makes art part of the everyday.

## Phased GTM Plan

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-8)

**Objective:** Build foundation, create assets, secure founding artist cohort, identify target venues.

**Actions:**

1. **Brand and web presence:**
   - Build simple, high-quality website (Squarespace or Webflow): homepage, how it works (artist), how it works (venue), application form, about/story.
   - Instagram account: start posting curated content (London venues, art in commercial spaces, photographer spotlights) 4-6 weeks before launch. Build to 500+ followers before inviting artists.
   - Define visual identity: clean, editorial, confident. Not "startup-y" -- more "independent gallery."

2. **Legal and operational setup:**
   - Company registered, agreements drafted, insurance in place (see Section 14).
   - Stripe account live, Airtable set up, workflow tools ready.

3. **Artist pipeline building:**
   - Identify 100-150 target photographers on Instagram (London-based or London-showing, 1k-50k followers, strong commercial aesthetic).
   - Begin engaging with their content (genuine comments, shares) 4 weeks before outreach.
   - Build a shortlist of 50-70 for direct outreach.

4. **Venue pipeline building:**
   - Walk East and South London target neighbourhoods: Peckham, Brixton, Hackney, Shoreditch, Bermondsey, Dalston, Deptford.
   - Identify 40-60 target venues (independent cafes and restaurants with good walls, decent footfall, design-conscious aesthetic).
   - Photograph their current walls (for before/after potential).
   - Note contact details, opening hours, manager names.

5. **Content creation:**
   - Write founding artist pitch (email template).
   - Write venue pitch (email + leave-behind card).
   - Create investor/advisor one-pager (not for fundraising, for credibility).
   - Prepare case study template (for use after first installations).

### Phase 1: Founding Artist Launch (Weeks 6-10)

**Objective:** Recruit 30-50 founding artists at Founding tier (£29/mo).

**The Offer:**
- "Founding Artist" status -- permanent rate lock at £29/mo (never increases for founding cohort).
- First access to London venue placements.
- Featured on launch campaign and website.
- Input into platform development.
- Limited to first 50 artists. Scarcity is real, not manufactured.

**Recruitment Strategy:**

1. **Direct Instagram outreach (primary channel):**
   - Personalised DMs to shortlisted photographers.
   - Template structure: genuine compliment on specific work, explain what Wallspace is (one line), explain the founding offer (one line), invite to apply or chat.
   - Target: message 50-70, expect 30-40 responses, convert 25-35 to applications, accept 20-30.
   - Follow up non-responses once at 5 days. No more.

2. **Referral seeding:**
   - Ask every accepted artist: "Who else should we invite?" Offer: if they refer someone who joins, both get first month free.
   - Target: 2-3 referrals per accepted artist.

3. **Photography community outreach:**
   - Post in relevant London photography groups (Facebook groups, Reddit r/london, photography forums).
   - Attend 2-3 photography meetups or exhibition openings. Talk to people. Hand out cards.
   - Contact London photography collectives and co-ops.

4. **Application process:**
   - Simple online form: name, Instagram, portfolio link, 5 best works, short statement, what tier interests them.
   - Review within 48 hours.
   - Acceptance email is warm, personal, and makes the artist feel selected (because they were).

**Messaging for Artists:**

*Primary message:* "Your photographs deserve to be on walls, not just on screens. Wallspace places your work in London's best independent venues -- cafes, restaurants, bars -- where real people see it every day. We handle curation, installation, signage, and sales. You create. We do the rest."

*Scarcity message:* "We're inviting 50 founding artists to launch with us. Founding members lock in the lowest rate permanently and get priority placement as we grow across London."

*Trust message:* "You keep ownership of your work. You set your prices. We display, we sell, we pay you. No exclusivity, no lock-in."

**What NOT to say:**
- Don't call it a "platform" or "marketplace" in artist-facing messaging. Say "curated programme" or "artist programme."
- Don't lead with the fee. Lead with the value (wall space, audience, sales).
- Don't promise sales. Promise exposure, professional presentation, and opportunity.

### Phase 2: Venue Pilot Launch (Weeks 8-14)

**Objective:** Onboard 10-15 pilot venues and complete first installations.

**The Offer:**
- Free curated artwork for your walls.
- Professionally installed and refreshed quarterly.
- Zero cost, zero effort, zero commitment beyond 30 days notice.
- "Launch partner" status -- featured on Wallspace website and social media.

**Recruitment Strategy:**

1. **In-person visits (primary -- no substitute):**
   - Visit target venues during quiet hours (2-4pm weekdays for cafes).
   - Ask to speak with owner or manager.
   - 60-second pitch: "I run a programme that puts curated artwork on cafe and restaurant walls for free. Professional photography, properly framed, installed by us, refreshed every quarter. No cost to you. Can I show you some examples?"
   - Show portfolio on phone or tablet (5-6 best examples of work that would suit their space).
   - If interested: book a 15-minute follow-up meeting or take their email.
   - If not available: leave a well-designed card with website URL and QR code.
   - Target: visit 40-50 venues, get 20-25 follow-up meetings, convert 10-15 to signed agreements.

2. **Warm introductions:**
   - Ask anyone in your network who knows independent venue owners in target areas.
   - Ask early-signed venues if they know other owners. Hospitality is a network business.

3. **Local business networks:**
   - Independent cafe/restaurant owner groups (Facebook, WhatsApp).
   - Local business associations (Peckham Coal Line, Brixton BID, etc.).
   - Not as primary channel -- supplements in-person visits.

**Messaging for Venues:**

*Primary message:* "Beautiful artwork. Zero cost. We handle everything."

*Expansion:* "We work with curated London photographers to display their work in independent venues like yours. The art is loaned for free, installed and refreshed by us, and comes with small cards that let your customers learn about and buy the pieces. Your walls look incredible. You pay nothing."

*Objection handling:*
- "What's the catch?" -- "There isn't one for you. Our artists pay a membership to be in the programme, and we take a small commission if a piece sells. You get beautiful walls for free."
- "We already have art." -- "That's great -- it shows you value your space. We offer professionally curated, rotating art so your space stays fresh without you having to think about it."
- "We don't want holes in our walls." -- "We use picture rail, adhesive hooks, or existing fixings wherever possible. We survey your space first and agree on everything before we touch a wall."
- "What if something sells and there's an empty wall?" -- "We replace sold pieces within a week. Your walls are never bare."

**What NOT to say to venues:**
- Don't make it sound complicated. "Free art, we handle everything" is the entire message.
- Don't lead with the business model or commission structure. They don't need to know the details.
- Don't oversell the "sales opportunity for your customers" angle -- most venues care about aesthetics, not becoming art dealers.

### Phase 3: First Installations and First Revenue (Weeks 12-20)

**Objective:** Complete first 10 installations. Generate first sales. Create proof.

**Actions:**

1. **Install systematically:**
   - Schedule 2-3 installations per week.
   - Founder does every installation personally.
   - Photograph everything: before (bare wall), during (installation), after (finished).
   - Post each installation on Instagram (tag venue and artists).

2. **Activate sales mechanism:**
   - QR codes live on every piece.
   - Purchase flow tested end-to-end before first venue goes live.
   - Monitor QR scans to understand engagement.

3. **Generate first case studies:**
   - After 2 weeks at each venue: check in with venue owner. How do customers react? Any comments? Any interest in specific pieces?
   - Capture quotes (even informal ones). "Customers love the new art" is gold.
   - Photograph customer engagement if possible (with permission).
   - Write up first case study: before/after photos, venue owner quote, artist perspective.

4. **First sale target:**
   - Realistically, first sale may take 4-8 weeks after installation.
   - Don't panic if sales are slow initially. The membership revenue sustains the business.
   - When first sale happens: celebrate publicly (with artist and venue permission). This is a milestone moment.
   - Process payout immediately and flawlessly. The first payout experience sets the tone.

5. **Feedback loop:**
   - Survey artists at 30 days: "How's your experience? What can we improve?"
   - Survey venues at 30 days: same questions.
   - Act on feedback quickly and visibly.

**Objective:** Reach 10 active venues with artwork. Prove the model works.

**Actions:**
- Continue venue outreach in target neighbourhoods.

### Phase 5: First 25 Venues (Months 5-9)

**Objective:** Scale to 25 venues. Prove repeatable operations. First hire consideration.

**Actions:**
- Expand geography: add new London neighbourhoods (Islington, Stoke Newington, Camberwell, Forest Hill, Walthamstow).
- Begin accepting mixed-media artists (painters, illustrators, printmakers) alongside photographers.
- Increase artist cohort to 75-100.
- Move to Core tier for new artists (£49/mo); Founding rate stays for existing.
- Develop venue referral programme: venues who refer another venue get a premium curation or featured event.
- Consider first part-time ops hire or VA.
- Build repeatable installation process that doesn't require founder for every install.

### Phase 6: First 100 Venues (Months 9-18)

**Objective:** Hit 100 venues. This is the inflection point for credibility, network effects, and potential investment.

**Actions:**
- Expand to all central and inner London.
- Begin conversations with multi-site groups (small chains of 3-10 venues).
- Launch Premium tier services (dedicated curation, priority refresh, event hosting).
- Build basic tech platform (artist dashboard, venue dashboard, catalogue).
- Consider fundraising if growth justifies it.
- Hire: 1 full-time ops person, 1 part-time installer.
- PR push: target national press, design publications, art world coverage.

---

### Phase 7: Multi-Site Groups (Month 12+)

**Objective:** Land first multi-site group client. This is where revenue scales.

**Actions:**
- Target: small chains (3-10 sites) in coffee, restaurant, or hotel sectors.
- Approach: in-person meeting with group operations or marketing director.
- Pitch: "You're paying an interior designer to pick generic prints. We provide curated, rotating original art at zero cost, with the added benefit of supporting local artists -- which your customers love."
- Offer: dedicated account management, site-specific curation, quarterly refresh, marketing collaboration.
- Revenue model: same (artist membership + sales commission), but volume creates efficiency.

## Content Strategy

### Instagram (Primary Social Channel)

**Posting cadence:** 4-5 posts per week, 3-5 stories per day during active periods.

**Content pillars:**

1. **Installation reveals (40%):** Before/after shots, fresh installations, venue transformations. This is the hero content. Always tag venue and artist.

2. **Artist spotlights (25%):** Short profile of a Wallspace artist. Their story, their work, why they joined. Humanises the programme. Creates content artists want to share.

3. **Venue features (15%):** Showcase the venue itself -- the food, the atmosphere, the people. This makes venues want to share and makes the partnership feel reciprocal.

4. **Behind the scenes (10%):** Installation process, curation decisions, packaging artwork, the van loaded up. Shows the work that goes into it. Builds founder credibility.

5. **Sales and customer moments (10%):** When a piece sells, celebrate it (with permission). Customer photos with their new artwork. Artist reactions.

**Hashtag strategy:**
- Branded: #wallspace #wallspacelondon #artonthewalls
- Discovery: #londonart #londonphotography #londoncafes #independentcafe #artforsale #photographyprints #londoncreatives #supportlocalartists

**Growth tactics:**
- Tag artists and venues in every relevant post (they reshare).
- Engage genuinely with London creative community accounts.
- Collaborate with food/cafe bloggers for cross-promotion.
- Run "guess the venue" or "which piece would you hang?" interactive stories.

### Website Content

- **Blog / Journal:** Monthly posts: artist interviews, venue stories, "art in your local" guides, photography tips, London neighbourhood art guides. SEO value builds over time.
- **Case studies:** Detailed write-ups of successful venue transformations. Include before/after photos, quotes, and any sales data. Target audience: future venues and potential investors.
- **Artist directory:** Searchable directory of all active artists. Each profile is a mini portfolio page. Good for artist SEO and customer discovery.

### Email Marketing

- **Artist newsletter:** Monthly. What's new, upcoming venues, tips, community highlights. Keep them engaged and feeling part of something.
- **Venue newsletter:** Quarterly. Programme updates, new artists, refresh reminders, success stories from other venues.
- **Customer list:** Build from QR scans and purchases. Monthly "new art in London" email featuring latest installations and available works.

## Local Network Strategy

### Neighbourhood Approach

Wallspace should feel like a local movement, not a faceless platform. Build neighbourhood by neighbourhood.

**For each target neighbourhood:**

1. **Map the scene:** Identify the 10-15 best independent venues, the local creative community, the existing art/photography events.
2. **Find the connectors:** Every neighbourhood has 2-3 people who know everyone. The cafe owner who's been there 15 years. The local photographer who runs meetups. The market organiser. Find them. Buy them a coffee.
3. **Get the first venue:** One venue signs up, and others notice. In a neighbourhood where owners talk to each other, one installation creates 3 conversations.
4. **Create a cluster:** Aim for 3-5 venues per neighbourhood. Dense enough that people notice Wallspace when they walk around. Creates a sense of "this is a Peckham thing" or "Hackney art trail."
5. **Local events:** After 3+ venues in a neighbourhood, host a "First Thursday" style art walk or late-night viewing event. Partner with venues for special menus or drinks.

### Key Relationships to Build

- Independent cafe/restaurant owner networks
- Local photography groups and collectives
- Art college alumni networks (Goldsmiths, Camberwell, Central Saint Martins)
- Co-working spaces (potential venues and artist connections)
- Local councils and BIDs (business improvement districts) -- some offer grants for creative placemaking
- Interior designers (potential referral partners for commercial spaces)

## Social Proof Strategy

### Before You Have Proof (Weeks 1-4)

- **Founder credibility:** Share your own background, passion, and expertise. Why are you qualified to curate art for venues?
- **Artist quality:** The calibre of your first artists IS your proof. Feature their existing work, exhibitions, publications.
- **Comparable models:** Reference similar models that work in other cities or countries (e.g., art lending libraries, gallery-cafe hybrids).
- **Vision:** Paint the picture. Show mockups of how their space would look. Use examples from similar venues in other contexts.

### Early Proof (Weeks 4-8)

- **Installation photos:** Every installation is a case study. Before and after shots are the single most powerful proof asset.
- **Venue owner quotes:** Even one line is enough. "It's transformed the space" or "customers love it."
- **Artist testimonials:** "I've never had my work on a wall outside my own studio before."
- **Numbers:** Share them even when small. "Launching with 30 curated artists across 5 London venues" sounds credible and exciting.

### Growing Proof (Months 2-6)

- **Sales data:** "Our artists have sold X works through venues" (share when numbers are meaningful).
- **Venue retention:** "100% of our pilot venues renewed for a second quarter."
- **Media mentions:** Feature any press coverage prominently.
- **Venue density:** "Now in 15 venues across Peckham, Hackney, and Brixton" creates FOMO for venues not yet involved.
- **Artist waitlist:** When you start getting more applications than you can accept, mention the acceptance rate. "Selected from over 200 applicants" is powerful.

### Scaled Proof (Months 6-12)

- **Case studies:** Full write-ups with data, photos, and quotes.
- **Venue testimonial video:** Short phone-filmed video of a venue owner talking about Wallspace. Authentic and powerful.
- **Artist success story:** Feature an artist who has sold multiple works through venues. Their journey and earnings.
- **Network map:** Visual showing all venue locations across London. Density = credibility.
- **Revenue milestones:** When appropriate, share aggregate artist earnings ("Our artists have earned over £X through Wallspace venues").

## Scarcity and Curation Positioning

Scarcity and curation are not marketing tricks for Wallspace -- they are operational realities. Use them honestly.

### Real scarcity points:
- You genuinely cannot accept every artist (some won't be good enough for commercial venues).
- You genuinely cannot onboard unlimited venues at launch (you're one person).
- Founding rate genuinely cannot be offered forever (it's below sustainable pricing).
- Wall space at each venue is genuinely limited.

### How to communicate curation:
- "We review every application personally."
- "Not every photographer is right for venue display -- we look for work that resonates in everyday spaces."
- "Our acceptance rate is X%." (Share when it's impressively selective, typically under 40%.)
- "Curated by Wallspace" should appear on all venue signage -- this is the quality stamp.

### How NOT to do it:
- Don't create fake urgency ("Only 3 spots left!" when there are 20).
- Don't reject artists just to lower the acceptance rate.
- Don't make venues feel like they're lucky to be chosen (they're doing you a favour by providing free wall space at launch).

## How to Get First Case Studies Fast

1. **Pick your 2-3 best installations** (best venue, best-looking artwork, best transformation).
2. **Visit at 2 weeks and 4 weeks** post-installation.
3. **Ask venue owner 3 questions:**
   - "What has the reaction been from customers?"
   - "Has it changed the feel of the space?"
   - "Would you recommend this to another venue owner?"
4. **Record their answers** (voice memo or notes -- don't make it formal).
5. **Take photos:** wide shot of the space, close-ups of artwork, any customer engagement you can capture.
6. **Write it up:** 300-500 words. Before photo. After photos. One or two quotes. Keep it authentic.
7. **Share:** Website blog post, Instagram carousel, send to local press.

**Target: first case study live by end of Month 1. Three case studies by end of Month 2.**

## Summary: The 5 Things That Matter Most for GTM

1. **Artist quality is everything.** If the art on the walls is mediocre, nothing else matters. Curate ruthlessly, even if it means launching with 20 artists instead of 50.

2. **In-person venue recruitment is non-negotiable.** No amount of email or social media replaces walking into a cafe and showing the owner what their walls could look like. Budget 2-3 days per week for venue visits in the first 2 months.

3. **Installation photos are your best marketing asset.** Before/after shots of a transformed venue sell the proposition to both future venues and future artists better than any copy.

4. **Move fast on proof.** The window between "interesting idea" and "we need to see evidence" is short. Get installations done, get feedback documented, get case studies written. Proof compounds.

5. **The founding cohort sets the culture.** Your first 30-50 artists define what Wallspace is. Choose people who are talented, professional, and enthusiastic. They become your ambassadors, your referral network, and your feedback loop. Invest disproportionate time in making their experience excellent.
