---

**Frequently asked questions**

**Is there a contract?**
No. Monthly rolling. Cancel any time with 30 days notice. We collect any displayed work within two weeks of cancellation.

**What if my work does not sell?**
The membership covers placement, not sales guarantees. Your work goes into venues with real footfall and real customers. Sales depend on the work, the price, and the venue. We provide pricing guidance and sales support, but we cannot guarantee outcomes.

**Can I change tiers?**
Yes. Upgrade or downgrade at any time. Changes take effect at the next billing cycle.

**What does the commission cover?**
Transaction processing, buyer communication, delivery coordination, and replacement logistics. You receive 80% of the sale price, paid within 14 days of the transaction.

**Do I need to frame my work?**
Work must be display-ready. If you need framing guidance or recommendations, we can help. Framing costs are your responsibility, but we have trade-rate contacts we can share.

## 10. Walk-In Script

This is the word-for-word script for walking into a venue cold.

---

**[Enter venue. Look around. Take note of the walls -- what is on them, what is not. Go to the bar or counter.]**

> "Hi there. Are you the owner, or is the owner around today?"

**If owner is not there:**

> "No worries. I run a company called Wallspace -- we supply free curated art for independent venues. I walked past and thought this space would be perfect. Could I leave a card and get the owner's name and email?"

**If owner is there:**

> "Hi, I am [Founder name]. I run Wallspace. We place curated original art and photography from local artists into independent venues like yours -- for free. I was in the area and your space caught my eye. Do you have two minutes?"

**If yes:**

> "The pitch is simple. We have a roster of vetted photographers and artists. We match their work to venues based on the space, the vibe, the clientele. We install everything professionally, rotate it every six to eight weeks so your walls stay fresh, and if a customer wants to buy a piece, we handle the sale. You get a share of any sales. The service costs you nothing."

**[Pause. Let them react.]**

**If they are interested:**

> "Great. What I would love to do is come back for a proper look at the space -- maybe take a few photos of the walls, understand what would work best here. Then I put together a proposal with some specific artists and pieces I think would suit. Takes about fifteen minutes. When would be a good time?"

**If they ask why it is free:**

> "Artists pay a monthly membership for the placement service. That covers the curation, installation, rotation, and sales support. You are the distribution channel -- your footfall is what makes the service valuable to artists. So it is genuinely free for you. No catch."

**If they are hesitant:**

> "Totally understand. Can I leave you a one-pager with all the details? No pressure. Have a read and if it sounds interesting, my number is on there. I am in [neighbourhood] regularly so I can always pop back."

**[Leave one-pager. Get name and email if possible. Thank them for their time. Leave.]**
