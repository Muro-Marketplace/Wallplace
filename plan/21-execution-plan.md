## F. CRITICAL DECISIONS TO MAKE NOW

These must be resolved before launch. Do not defer them.

### Decision 1: Company Structure
**Decide**: Sole trader or Limited company?
**Recommendation**: Limited company. Protects personal assets, looks more professional to venues, and is required for most business insurance policies.
**Decide by**: Week 1.

### Decision 2: Founding Tier Cap
**Decide**: How many artists get the £29/month Founding rate, and when does it close?
**Recommendation**: Cap at 50 artists or 90 days from launch, whichever comes first. Communicate this cap publicly from day one to create urgency.
**Decide by**: Week 1.

### Decision 3: Venue Revenue Share
**Decide**: Do venues get a cut of sales made in their space?
**Recommendation**: Yes. Offer 10% of the sale price to the venue. This aligns incentives — venues will actively point customers to the art and mention it's for sale. The remaining split is 70% to artist, 20% to Wallspace.
**Decide by**: Week 2 (before first venue pitch).

### Decision 4: Art Pricing Authority
**Decide**: Who sets the price of artwork — the artist or Wallspace?
**Recommendation**: The artist sets the price, but Wallspace provides pricing guidance and has the right to decline pieces priced outside the recommended venue range (£100–600). Artists who insist on gallery-level pricing for a cafe context will create friction.
**Decide by**: Week 2 (before artist onboarding).

### Decision 5: Damage and Loss Liability
**Decide**: Who is liable if artwork is damaged or stolen in a venue?
**Recommendation**: Wallspace carries public liability insurance. Venue agreement includes a damage clause requiring the venue to take reasonable care. Artist agreement includes an acknowledgment that display carries inherent risk. For high-value pieces (over £500), require the artist to maintain their own insurance.
**Decide by**: Week 2 (before any artwork leaves an artist's possession).

### Decision 6: Exclusivity
**Decide**: Are artists exclusive to Wallspace, or can they sell through other channels simultaneously?
**Recommendation**: Non-exclusive. Artists can sell anywhere. Wallspace is an additional channel, not a replacement. This reduces sign-up friction significantly. If specific pieces are currently displayed in a Wallspace venue, those pieces should not be listed elsewhere during the display period.
**Decide by**: Week 2 (before artist agreement is finalised).

### Decision 7: Payment Timing for Artist Payouts
**Decide**: When do artists receive their share of a sale?
**Recommendation**: Within 7 business days of receiving cleared funds from the customer. Communicate this clearly in the artist agreement. Delays in artist payment erode trust faster than almost anything else.
**Decide by**: Week 3 (before first sale is possible).

### Decision 8: Minimum Display Period
**Decide**: How long must artwork remain in a venue before rotation?
**Recommendation**: 4-week minimum display period. This gives art a fair chance to be seen and purchased, while keeping venues engaged with regular fresh content. Venues can request early removal if there is a genuine issue (e.g., customer complaint about content).
**Decide by**: Week 3.

## G. WHAT CAN BE DEFERRED

These items feel urgent but are not. Defer them without guilt.

| Item | Why It Feels Urgent | Why It Can Wait | Defer Until |
|------|-------------------|----------------|-------------|
| Building a marketplace platform / app | "We need technology to scale" | You need 50 paying artists and 20 venues before technology creates any leverage. Manual processes teach you what to build. | Month 6–9 |
| Artist dashboard / login portal | "Artists need to see their placements and sales" | A monthly email update or WhatsApp message works fine at this scale. Dashboard adds zero value under 50 artists. | Month 6+ |
| Automated matching algorithm | "We should match art to venues intelligently" | The founder's taste and judgment is the matching algorithm for now. It's better than any algorithm at this stage because it accounts for nuance. | Month 9+ |
| SEO optimisation | "We need organic traffic" | SEO takes 6–12 months to yield results. Your time is better spent on direct outreach. | Month 4+ |
| Email marketing automation | "We need drip campaigns for onboarding" | You're onboarding artists one by one with personal calls. That converts better than any email sequence. | Month 4+ |
| Blog content | "Content marketing drives long-term growth" | True, but irrelevant when you have 15 artists. Write one foundational post, then focus on Instagram. | Month 3+ |
| Hiring anyone | "I need help" | You probably do, but you can't afford to hire wrong. Do everything yourself first so you know exactly what to delegate. | Month 4–6 (part-time handler), Month 9+ (ops) |
| Expanding beyond photography | "Painters keep asking to join" | Every new medium adds complexity — framing requirements, weight limits, installation differences. Master photography first. | Month 6+ |
| Expanding beyond East/South London | "There's a great cafe in Islington" | Geographic spread kills operational efficiency. One cafe in Islington means a 45-minute trip for every installation and rotation. | Month 4+ |
| Press and PR | "If we get featured in Time Out, we'll blow up" | Press creates a spike, not a sustained funnel. You can't service a spike of demand right now anyway. | Month 3+ |
| Partnerships with art schools | "They have hundreds of potential artists" | Institutional partnerships take months to formalise and yield low-quality leads. DM individual graduates directly instead. | Month 6+ |
| Corporate venue partnerships | "Offices would love this" | Different buyer, different sales cycle, different art requirements. Stay consumer-facing until the model is proven. | Month 9+ |
| Formal brand guidelines document | "We need brand consistency" | A logo, two colours, and one font is enough. You can see every piece of content yourself at this size. | Month 6+ |
| Financial modelling and forecasting | "Investors will want projections" | You don't need investors. You need customers. The only forecast that matters is next month's target. | Month 6+ |
| VAT registration | "We might need to register" | Only mandatory at £90,000 turnover. You won't hit that in Year 1 (base case). Monitor quarterly and register when approaching the threshold. | Month 9+ or when turnover approaches £85k |

## Summary: The First 90 Days in One Sentence Per Phase

**Days 1–10**: Build the legal, financial, and operational foundation. Register the company, get insurance, set up payments, build the website.

**Days 11–20**: Start talking to real artists and real venues. Send DMs, visit cafes, collect applications, pitch owners.

**Days 21–30**: Convert conversations into commitments. Sign first paying artists, sign first venue partners, complete first installation.

**Days 31–60**: Prove the model works. Install in 5+ venues, process first sales, confirm artists see value, confirm venues stay engaged.

**Days 61–90**: Scale what's working, fix what's not. Expand to 12+ venues, grow to 35+ artists, close the Founding tier, begin referral programme, conduct full Q1 review.
